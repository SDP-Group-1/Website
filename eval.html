Evaluation

Fall detection

ML model:

As the original dataset is extremely imbalanced, the ratio of the minority (falls event) and the majority of samples (normal behaviors) are 0.0576, therefore, evaluation by the commonly used accuracy is meaningless and misleading. By our further study, we choose F1- score as our main metric to evaluate the performance of the model.

As F1-score values equally on precision and recall, in our case, we expect the model can capture all the potential fall events, to reach this aim, we need a model with recall (sensitivity) as high as possible, and also a relatively high precision to reduce false alarm. So, our basic methodology is that firstly through the value of F1-score, we could get an intuitive reflection on how the model performs and by further checking the precision and sensitivity to determine whether the model should be adopted. The final result on average F1-score is 0.941 (10-fold stratified), and the corresponded recall and precision respectively 0.998 and 0.891. App:

Evaluation on fall-detection functionality of App :As in this demo, we have integrated the App interface with the fall-detection backend, therefore, we are able to do quantitative tests on different scenarios, and notice that these tests mainly aim to test the usability of the fall-detection function but not the reliability or correctness(to prove these we obviously need to do more systematic tests with more scenarios and trial times).The test results contained two scenarios are shown.
And the first scenario is that physically drop the fall from a certain height with a pad on the ground as a buffer. The second scenario is that carrying the phone (in pocket) and do 4 types of fall trials which contains:
FOL:
Fall forward from standing, use of hands to dampen fall
FKL:
Fall forward from standing, first impact on knees
BSC:
Fall backward while trying to sit on a chair
SDL:
Fall side wards from standing, bending legs

As the result indicates, the performance of the fall detection classifier is not good as our expectation, specifically, we find that the data we acquired from our App is incompatible with the data we used for training (MobiAct), which might be the reason for it. In many our trials, some feature, for example the maximum SMVs within a window period (we explained it in the demo before) is greater than that value of we extracted from the Whole training dataset. And we also find hint in the research (Casilari et al., 2017), in which Casilari et al. compares different public dataset for fall detection system, and they illustrate as the figure below: We can see from this figure that the value of this feature is generally lower than it of other dataset. To solve this incompatible issue, we need to combine other public dataset or to collect data by ourselves.








Hardware and Navigation

Hardware:

To measure the sturdiness of the frame stress tests were done with a load of 185lbs which is the full weight of a 70-year-old male in the 95th percentile of elderly people aged 60-80. Since the robot will never carry the user at once, this load is more than sufficient for testing purposes. The team has conducted an analysis on 3 different joints using 3 different metrics: 1st Principal Stress which helps understand the maximum tensile stress, Constraints and Load and Displacement.

Navigation:

The building of the occupancy grid was running multiple times in different environments using different parameters. We tested the number of scans needed to produce a good occupancy grid. We found that the bare minimum to visit all parts of the room was best. Too many scans would produce incomprehensible occupancy grids as can be seen in the figure.
